Diebolt-Vallois

Cramant · Côte des Blancs

Based in the legendary Grand Cru village of Cramant, Diebolt-Vallois produces classic, pure, and intensely chalky Blanc de Blancs Champagnes. Under Jacques Diebolt and his children, the domaine has built an elite reputation for capturing the absolute structural precision of old-vine Chardonnay planted across Côte des Blancs limestone.

Why Wine Bank

Benchmark grower Champagne. The Fleur de Passion is an absolute masterpiece of old-vine purity raised completely in barrel without malolactic fermentation.

Dosnon

Avirey-Lingey · Côte des Bar

Nicolas Dosnon crafts intensely textured, winelike Champagnes in the Kimmeridgian clay-limestone hills of the Aube. Strongly influenced by Puligny-Montrachet vinification methods, all base juices are fermented and aged in used Puligny oak barrels, imbuing the final wines with spectacular breadth, structure, and mineral density.

Why Wine Bank

For lovers of vinous, food-friendly Champagne. The combination of dense Pinot Noir fruit and seasoned white Burgundy oak delivers deep complexity.

Pierre Paillard

Bouzy · Montagne de Reims

Brothers Antoine and Quentin Paillard run this exceptional 11-hectare estate exclusively inside the Grand Cru terroir of Bouzy. Known globally for subverting the heavy, monolithic stereotype of Bouzy Pinot Noir, they use meticulous viticulture and low-dosage levels to produce single-parcel cuvées showing immense vibrance, chalky definition, and focus.

Why Wine Bank

Rarely does Bouzy display this much cut and freshness. Don't skip their rare still Bouzy Rouge, a tiny-production Pinot Noir of immense clarity.

Bruno Clavelier

Vosne-Romanée · Côte de Nuits

Bruno Clavelier is one of Burgundy’s most intellectual and deeply respected winemakers. A pioneer of certified biodynamic farming, his focus is entirely geared toward old-vine massal selections and parsing the intricate geologic layers of his soils. Using very minimal new oak and delicate extractions, Bruno lets the terroir speak with transparent, silk-textured clarity.

Why Wine Bank

A true connoisseur's domaine. His parcels in Vosne-Romanée and Chambolle are models of stunning perfume, structure, and long-term evolutionary potential.

Domaine Bernard-Bonin

Meursault · Côte de Beaune

Run by Véronique Bonin and Nicolas Bernard, this tiny 7-hectare estate has exploded into one of the most cult-followed, heavily allocated white Burgundy properties on earth. Utilizing meticulous biodynamic farming, early harvests, no sulfur additions until bottling, and expansive barrel aging, they create wildly energetic, crystalline, and brilliantly chiseled expressions of Meursault.

Why Wine Bank

Among the hardest-to-source cult white Burgundies in existence. Masterful tension, reduction, and deeply concentrated palate energy.

Éric Forest

Vergisson · Mâconnais

Farming high-altitude parcels on the striking limestone cliffs of Vergisson, Éric Forest channels the soul of precision winemaking. Mentored directly by the iconic Jean-Marie Guffens, Éric applies incredible dynamic handling in the cellar, picking by hand and barrel fermenting to create complex, intensely saline, and mineral-driven Pouilly-Fuissé.

Incredible value. These wines rival top-tier Côte de Beaune premier crus at a mere fraction of the price.

Domaine Bruno Lorenzon

Mercurey · Côte Chalonnaise

Bruno Lorenzon has single-handedly elevated Mercurey onto the world stage. An uncompromising purist, his high-density vineyards are farmed organically with absolute obsession. In the cellar, Bruno uses custom-made oak barrels with no toast, producing pure, chiseled, incredibly vibrant reds and whites that possess staggering architectural tension.

Why Wine Bank

An insider favorite that regularly outperforms far more prestigious appellations in blind tastings. Exceptional structure and terroir transparency.
